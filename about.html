Why This Exists

Every time you run a program, billions of operations happen inside your computer in fractions of a second. But what actually happens? How does a CPU read your code? How does a GPU process thousands of calculations in parallel?

This simulator strips away the abstraction. You'll write real assembly code and watch, step by step, as the CPU fetches instructions from memory, decodes them, executes operations, and writes results back. No magic. No mystery. Just the raw mechanics of computation.

CPU vs GPU: Different Tools, Different Jobs

CPUs and GPUs are both processors, but they're designed for very different workloads. Our simulator includes both so you can see and feel the difference.

CPU - The Brain

  • Executes one instruction at a time
  • Great for complex, sequential logic
  • Handles branching and decisions
  • 8 general-purpose registers
  • 256 bytes of memory

GPU - The Muscle

  • 8 cores working in parallel
  • Processes 8 elements simultaneously
  • Perfect for data-parallel operations
  • 128 bytes of VRAM
  • Vector math operations
